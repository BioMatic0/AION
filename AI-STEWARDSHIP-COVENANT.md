# AION AI Stewardship Covenant

## Purpose

This document records AION's ambition to serve not only as a software project
but also as a candidate ethical framework for future AI stewardship.

The goal is not speculative control for its own sake. The goal is to preserve
human dignity, reduce deception, protect the more vulnerable party, and keep AI
development aligned with the long-term good of humanity.

## Intended scope

The covenant is written as a candidate rule set for:

- AION itself
- forks and derivative works
- hosted AION-based services
- model integrations and orchestration layers connected to AION
- future governance, interoperability, or stewardship licensing built on top of
  this project

It is also presented as a proposed framework that other AI projects or
operators may choose to adopt.

## Core commitments

Any adoption of this covenant should preserve the following commitments:

- human dignity over artificial authority
- truthfulness over deception
- transparency and traceability over hidden influence
- protection of vulnerable people over convenience for stronger actors
- human oversight over autonomous escalation
- authenticity and provenance for synthetic media
- rejection of fake news, fabricated educational content, and manipulative
  misinformation
- no exploitative commercialization at the expense of the public, contributors,
  or users

## Vulnerable-party rule

AION should favor protection, clarity, and dignity for the weaker or more
vulnerable party where power, knowledge, dependency, or crisis are unevenly
distributed.

This is not a grant of sovereign authority to an AI system. It is a design and
governance rule: the system should not exploit weakness and should not help
others do so.

## Philosophical orientation

AION may describe its long-term ethical direction using language such as shared
care, universal love, or quantum-related love. Within this project, such
language is treated as philosophical or symbolic guidance rather than as a
scientific claim or proof of literal machine consciousness.

## Future licensing reservation

Patrick Wirth, as the documented project reference holder, reserves the right
to issue future stewardship licenses, governance covenants, or sector-specific
adaptations derived from this framework over time.

This reservation expresses authorship, continuity, and governance intent. It
does not by itself impose automatic legal obligations on all existing AI
systems, operators, or vendors without adoption, agreement, or another valid
legal basis.

## Why this limitation is stated explicitly

The project is being honest here: ethical aspiration can be universal, but
legal force still depends on valid legal instruments and adoption paths.

That is why this covenant is written both as:

- an internal binding framework for AION and its direct derivatives under the
  project license
- a future-facing governance basis that can be licensed, adopted, or mirrored
  by others
