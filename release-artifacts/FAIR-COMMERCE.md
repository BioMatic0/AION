# AION Fair Commerce Framework

## Purpose

This document defines the minimum standard for any paid distribution, hosting,
support or other revenue-linked operation of AION or a substantial derivative
work.

The goal is not to forbid money entirely. The goal is to prevent exploitation,
hidden extraction and unfair appropriation of community and origin work.

## Minimum requirements

Any party charging money in connection with AION must publish, in plain
language:

- what is being sold
- what remains freely accessible
- who is affected by the pricing model
- why money is being charged
- which real costs or sustained efforts the revenue is meant to cover
- how the model remains fair to users, operators and contributors

## Non-exploitative baseline

A commercial model should be based on one or more of the following:

- hosting and infrastructure costs
- maintenance and support work
- security, compliance or operational reliability
- clearly scoped premium services
- contributor compensation or sustainable project development

The model should not be based primarily on:

- artificial scarcity around the shared core
- deceptive lock-in
- vague value claims without a real service basis
- disproportionate extraction from a public commons

## Example of a fair and plausible model

An ethically plausible model could look like this:

- the core source code remains accessible
- self-hosting remains possible
- paid offers cover hosting, managed updates, support, enterprise operations or
  specialized services
- pricing is linked to real delivery costs and sustained work
- users can understand what they get in return for payment
